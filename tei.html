TEI Output¶

TEI is a consortium maintaining standards for the representation of texts in digital form which are widely used in the humanities. Nidaba is capable of encoding the OCR results and their metadata into XML documents following the most recent P5 guidelines. The output is designed to facilitate further manual annotation.

Format¶

Fundamentally output is generated following the embedded transcription scheme with a single surface containing all recognized line elements with their textual representation. When merging the output of multiple OCR engines diverging readings will be attributed to their origin using the respStmt tag. Alternative spellings provided by a spell checker will also be properly attributed.

The skeleton of a TEI-OCR file will look like this:

<sourceDoc>
        <surface ulx="0" uly="0" lrx="500" lry="500">
                <graphic url="foo.png"/>
                <zone>
                        <line ulx=a uly=b lrx=c lry=d>text...</line>
                        ...
                </zone>
        </surface>
</sourceDoc>
