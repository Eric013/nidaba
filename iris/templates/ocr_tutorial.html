Scan Requirements

In order for the OCR engine to properly analyze your scans, they must meet certain requirements.

You can use any scanner that supports a high enough resolution. Each scanner's settings interface will vary by manufacturer and model. Here are some suggested settings which are standard in most scanning interfaces. The critial components are:

  1. Document-format scanning (if available)
  2. Color Mode
  3. Resolution (DPI) between 400 and 600
  4. PNG, TIFF, or Pyramidal TIFF format



Already have scans? Go through the steps below to make sure your scans are suitable for OCR.

Resolution must be between 400 and 600 DPI

Dots per inch (DPI) is a measure of dot density, and indicates the level of detail your scan has.

Check the DPI of your Scans (Mac)
  1. Open your scan in Preview. If double-clicking a file does not automatically open Preview, you can locate preview by opening Finder » Applications » Preview
  2. In Preview's top toolbar, navigate to Tools » Show Inspector. (See Screenshot)
  3. The resulting pop-up window indicates the image's DPI.

Note You cannot change the DPI to a higher number and retain image quality. You must set the DPI to a high enough setting during the intial scanning process. If the DPI of your images is too low, you will need to re-scan your images. See Choosing your scanner's settings.


Check the DPI of your Scans (Windows)

Scan in Greyscale or Color Mode

Scan must be in Pyramidal TIFF, TIFF, or PNG format

Do not convert JPG scans to PNG or TIFF formats. Conversation algorithms will not improve the quality but, contrarily, cause data loss.

If your scans are not in one of these accepted formats, you will need to rescan your documents. See Choosing your scanner's settings for more information.
